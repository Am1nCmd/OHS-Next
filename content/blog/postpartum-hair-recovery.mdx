Pregnancy keeps hair in a prolonged anagen (growth) phase. After delivery, hormones recalibrate and a cohort of follicles that "should have" shed during those nine months all reach telogen at roughly the same time. The result is telogen effluvium — a dramatic, alarming, but usually self-limiting shed.

## The typical timeline

- **Months 1–2** — minimal change for most mothers.
- **Months 3–5** — shedding peaks. Hair comes out in the shower, on pillows, in brushes. A parting that looks "see-through" in photos is common.
- **Months 6–9** — the shed slows, regrowth begins. You'll notice soft baby hairs along the hairline.
- **Month 12** — in a typical recovery, most of the density has returned, though full texture may take another six months.

## When it's worth a consult

If by month seven you're still shedding at peak levels, or if regrowth is absent, it's worth investigating. Common contributors beyond hormones include:

- **Iron and ferritin depletion** from pregnancy and labour.
- **Thyroid shifts** — postpartum thyroiditis is underdiagnosed.
- **Nutritional gaps** from breastfeeding without proportionate intake.

A trichologist can map your shed pattern and, in collaboration with your GP if needed, rule out drivers that won't resolve on their own.

## Gentle care during the wave

- Wash as often as you like — not washing doesn't reduce shedding, it just delays the visible evidence.
- Skip tight styles while recovery is in progress.
- A scalp massage a few times a week supports microcirculation and, honestly, is one of the few acts of self-care that fits into the first year.
