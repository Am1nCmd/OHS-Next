The scalp is a living ecosystem — a biofilm of sebum, commensal bacteria, and keratinocytes that functions best within a narrow band of temperature and pH. Singapore's climate pushes it toward the edges of that band for most of the year.

## The three forces at play

**Ambient humidity** keeps sweat and sebum sitting on the scalp for longer, softening the stratum corneum and inviting opportunistic *Malassezia* overgrowth.

**Chronic sun exposure** — even indirect — raises scalp surface temperature and accelerates oxidative stress on follicular cells.

**Product migration** from face sunscreen and haircare creams drifts into the hairline and builds up at the parting, where it occludes pores.

## A climate-matched routine

1. **Cleanse with intent.** A weekly deep-cleansing herbal rinse (we recommend a warm decoction, not a clay mask) lifts build-up without stripping the acid mantle.
2. **Condition mid-length down only.** Keep heavy conditioners away from the scalp; they contribute more to oiliness than most people realise.
3. **Shield without occluding.** Use a lightweight mineral SPF designed for the hairline — spray products migrate less than creams.
4. **Rebalance monthly.** A trichoscope review every 4–6 weeks tells you whether your routine is holding or whether your scalp is drifting.

None of this is glamorous. All of it compounds. A well-maintained scalp biome is the foundation any growth programme is built on.
