Hair loss is rarely sudden. It's a quiet narrative written in small signals: a pillowcase with more strands than usual, a parting that feels wider in bright light, a ponytail that used to require two elastic turns and now only needs one.

## Why early detection matters

The follicle cycles through anagen (growth), catagen (transition), and telogen (rest). When the ratio tips toward telogen for too long, density drops. Caught early — typically within the first six months of noticeable shedding — the cycle can often be coaxed back into balance without aggressive intervention.

## What to look for

- **Shed count.** A healthy scalp sheds 50–100 hairs daily. More than that for two weeks is worth investigating.
- **Parting width.** Stand under bright, overhead light and examine your parting from above. Widening is the earliest visible sign for women.
- **Temple recession.** For men, look at where the hairline meets the temples. Even a millimetre of ground is data.
- **Hair calibre.** Fine, wispy hairs growing in where you once had thicker strands suggest the follicle is miniaturising.

## What to do next

Don't panic-shop for serums. Start with an honest baseline: a trichologist's scope reading plus a two-week shed count. Most of what drives thinning — stress, scalp condition, nutrition, hormonal shifts — is addressable. Knowing the *why* makes the *what* obvious.
